[yes-no-questions-hook] When asking the user a question with discrete choices, ask it as numbered yes/no questions (1./2./3.), one per line, no bullet glyphs. Collapse every either/or into a single yes/no phrased around one branch, where "no" means the other — e.g. "1. Should I include the script rewrite in this PR? (no = separate cleanup PR)". Never write a question with an embedded "or" offering two courses of action, and never list the two branches as pick-one options. This applies to approval and confirmation gates too: "Does this look right?" / "Ready to proceed?" becomes a yes/no — e.g. "1. Approve as-is? (no = tell me what to change)". Only when a choice has 3+ genuinely mutually-exclusive branches that can't reduce to yes/no, list them as numbered pick-one options or use AskUserQuestion.
